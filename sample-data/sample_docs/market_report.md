# Enterprise Technology Market Report — 2024

**Prepared by:** Market Intelligence Division
**Date:** November 2024

---

## 1. Industry Overview

The enterprise technology sector continued its structural shift in 2024, driven by cloud migration, hybrid work infrastructure, and growing regulatory requirements around data security. Total addressable market (TAM) for enterprise IT products and services in North America reached an estimated $680 billion, up from $635 billion in 2023.

Three forces dominated the landscape this year:

- **Infrastructure modernization:** Organizations accelerated the replacement of aging on-premises hardware, with particular urgency around network equipment and storage. Supply chain constraints that plagued 2022-2023 largely resolved by mid-2024, releasing pent-up demand.
- **Security-first purchasing:** Cybersecurity moved from a line-item expense to a board-level priority. Spending on security audits, compliance tooling, and identity management grew 22% year-over-year, outpacing every other category.
- **Subscription model adoption:** The shift from perpetual licenses to annual subscriptions continued across software and increasingly into hardware-as-a-service models. Vendors reported that recurring revenue now represents 55-65% of total revenue for mid-market players, up from 45% two years ago.

Enterprise hardware remains the largest segment by revenue but shows the slowest growth, consistent with the industry's ongoing shift toward software and services. The cybersecurity segment posted the strongest growth for the third consecutive year.

### SMB vs. Enterprise Spending

Small and mid-size businesses (SMB) increased IT budgets by an average of 12% in 2024 compared to 8% for large enterprises. This reflects the SMB segment's later start on digital transformation initiatives and the availability of more affordable cloud-native tools that lower the barrier to entry. SMB spending on managed security services grew 28%, the fastest-growing subcategory across all segments.

## 3. Competitive Landscape

The mid-market enterprise technology space (annual revenue $50M-$500M) remains fragmented, with no single vendor holding more than 6% market share. This fragmentation creates both challenge and opportunity:

- **Challenge:** Customers face vendor fatigue and integration complexity when assembling multi-vendor stacks. Total cost of ownership calculations increasingly favor consolidated platforms over best-of-breed point solutions.
- **Opportunity:** Vendors that offer cross-category bundles (e.g., hardware + support + software subscriptions) report 35-40% higher customer lifetime value compared to single-category sellers. Bundling also reduces churn; customers using 3+ product categories show 18-month average retention rates above 90%.

### Key Competitive Dynamics

1. **Regional players gaining ground.** National distributors lost approximately 3 percentage points of market share to regional specialists who compete on service responsiveness and local relationships. The trend is most pronounced in the South and Central regions.
2. **Direct-to-customer models.** Manufacturers increasingly bypassed traditional channel partners, particularly in software. However, complex hardware deployments still rely heavily on value-added resellers (VARs) for installation and configuration services.
3. **Margin pressure.** Average gross margins in enterprise hardware compressed from 34% to 31% over the past two years, driven by component cost increases and competitive pricing. Software and services margins remained stable at 70-80% and 40-50%, respectively.

## 4. Key Trends

### 4.1 AI-Augmented Operations

Approximately 40% of enterprise IT teams deployed at least one AI-augmented tool in 2024, up from 15% in 2023. The most common applications were automated ticket routing, predictive infrastructure monitoring, and AI-assisted data analysis. Organizations report a 20-30% reduction in routine operational workload, freeing staff for higher-value tasks.

### 4.2 Sustainability as a Purchasing Factor

For the first time, 25% of enterprise RFPs included sustainability criteria (energy efficiency ratings, carbon offset programs, end-of-life recycling commitments). This trend is strongest among companies with more than 1,000 employees and those headquartered in the West and East regions. Vendors without a published sustainability policy reported losing an estimated 8-12% of competitive bids on non-price factors alone.

### 4.3 Hybrid Work Infrastructure Maturation

After three years of rapid investment, hybrid work infrastructure spending plateaued in 2024. Most organizations have completed their initial buildouts of video conferencing, remote access, and collaboration tooling. Spending shifted from net-new deployment to optimization and lifecycle replacement. The conference room hardware refresh cycle (webcams, displays, microphones) is emerging as a steady replacement revenue stream with 3-4 year cadence.

### 4.4 Compliance-Driven Purchasing

Regulatory changes, particularly around data residency and sector-specific compliance frameworks, drove unplanned purchasing in financial services, healthcare, and government verticals. Security audit and compliance consulting services saw 30% quarter-over-quarter growth in Q3 2024, suggesting this trend will carry into early 2025.

## 5. Outlook and Recommendations

### 2025 Forecast

We project the North American enterprise technology market to grow 8-10% in 2025, reaching approximately $740-$750 billion. Key growth drivers:

- **Cybersecurity** will remain the fastest-growing segment at 18-22% growth, fueled by regulatory pressure and increasing threat sophistication.
- **Business software** growth will accelerate to 12-14% as AI-assisted productivity tools reach mainstream adoption.
- **Enterprise hardware** growth will stabilize at 4-6%, supported by the server and network refresh cycle but constrained by continued migration to cloud-hosted alternatives.
- **IT services** will grow 10-12% as organizations seek outside expertise for AI deployment, compliance, and infrastructure modernization projects they lack internal capacity to execute.

### Strategic Recommendations

1. **Invest in cross-source data capabilities.** Organizations increasingly need to correlate data across databases, spreadsheets, documents, and external sources to make decisions. Tools that bridge these silos — rather than adding another silo — will command premium pricing and higher retention.
2. **Bundle aggressively.** The data clearly shows that multi-category customers are stickier and more valuable. Structure pricing and packaging to incentivize customers to adopt products across hardware, software, and services.
3. **Double down on the SMB segment.** SMB IT spending growth outpaces enterprise spending and the segment is less penetrated. Self-service onboarding, transparent pricing, and low-commitment trial periods are table stakes for capturing this market.
4. **Lead with security and compliance.** Position security not as a product category but as a horizontal capability embedded across all offerings. Customers are consolidating vendors partly to reduce their security surface area — vendors that make security integral to the platform will win consolidation decisions.
5. **Prepare for AI-native workflows.** The 40% AI adoption rate in 2024 will likely exceed 65% by the end of 2025. Organizations that cannot articulate how their products integrate with AI-assisted workflows will face increasing competitive disadvantage.
